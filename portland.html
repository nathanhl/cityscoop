Portland

Brews, coffees, and waterfalls

Welcome to Portland

Portland, Oregon’s largest city, sits on the Columbia and Willamette rivers, in the shadow of snow-capped Mount Hood. It’s known for its parks, bridges and bicycle paths, as well as for its eco-friendliness and its microbreweries and coffeehouses. Iconic Washington Park encompasses sites from the formal Japanese Garden to Oregon Zoo and its railway. The city hosts thriving art, theater and music scenes.
- Wikipedia

Cold Brew @ Heart Coffee Roasters

This small cafe has one of the best cold-brews I've ever had. The fresh-roasted beans are a little pricey, but they're definitely worth the price you pay. I got the Kenyan coffee, and I wish I'd brought another bag.

Beware of Spiders @ International Rose Test Garden

Be careful when you stick your nose into the roses when you smell them! It turns out spiders like to hang out in the center of the blossoms to catch whatever insects that stop by to pollinate them. Check before you sniff!

Beer Flight @ Deschutes Brewery

The flight from Deschutes is a must-do! Ten beers, some better than others, but all guaranteed to give you a good night.

Thai Food @ Pok Pok

Pok Pok is a must try in Portland. The chicken wings are sticky and spicy, and the papaya salad is fresh and crunchy. Definitely opt into the sticky rice, as well. Some locations are less crowded than others, so just call ahead and make sure you're not waiting two hours for a table.

Box of Doughnuts @ Blue Star Doughnuts

I stopped to pick one doughnut up for breakfast, but I couldn't resist buying a whole box for myself. The flavors just looked too amazing. The blueberry bourbon basil was to die for.

Bridge Photos @ Multnomah Falls

Oregon has one of the most beautiful scenif views in the entire world. Green, lush, and free-flowing; it's also free!

Khao Man Gai @ Nong's Khao Man Gai

This place does the Thai staple almost as good as the street vendors in Thailand. Tender chicken meets fragrant rice, and the sauce is life-changing. Be prepared to wait a bit though, because the lines can get long, even at the food stall.

Clown Head @ Random House

It's just creepy, really. People need to stop with the clown obsessions.

Rock Throne @ Multnomah

Find a random pile of rocks in the woods, and look cute. Make everything your prop, because you're a model and you deserve it LOL

Waterfall Showers @ Latourell Falls

You can actually walk right under this waterfall, if you're brave enough. Even in the summer the water is ice cold, and will sure to drench you to your core.

Cocktails @ Teardrop Lounge

The drinks are strong, and masterfully crafted. I do have some qualms about the decor being borderline kitschy, but it's something I can deal with because the drinks are delicious.

Fresh Lavender @ PSU Weekend Farmer's Market

Everything at this farmer's market is fantastic. The cherries and peaches are fresh and sweet, the produce is vibrant and delicious. But the true star is the gentle fragrance of fresh lavendar wafting from a couple of the stalls. What a way to start the weekend!

Beer and Food @ Ex Novo Breweries

The beer was innovative, the food was delicious, and the outside seating made for a perfect summer evening. The totchos were amazing, and the special mahi mahi tacos were refreshing as well. Don't sit inside in the summer, it's too stuffy.

Creme Brulee Doughnut @ Blue Star Doughnuts

If you like creme brulee, you'll love this doughnut. It even has a little pipette of cointreau that you can squeeze into the pastry before you eat it. It is really sweet though, so be careful if you're not into that!

Sandwiches @ Lardo

The sandwiches at Lardo are legendary, especially the specialty sandwiches of the month. July's was the Zombie, with pastrami and coleslaw on rye bread. They do offer a porchetta sandwich, but that has been restricted only to the weekends.

Coffee @ Coava Coffee Roasters

Coffee at this hollowed out factory is fantastic. I loved people watching here, and their Los Nacientes Coffee from Costa Rica has been my go-to coffee for the past couple months!
